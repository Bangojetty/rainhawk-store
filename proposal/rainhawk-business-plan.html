02 — Market & Positioning

Why Rainhawk, why now

The shift

Outdoor apparel is no longer confined to the mountains — it's mainstream lifestyle wear. Buyers want gear that performs in real weather and looks good at the coffee shop. Sun hoodies, packable shells, and trail-ready accessories are crossing over into everyday wardrobes.

Our wedge

Rainhawk owns a sharp, memorable position: "we head out anyway." Weather-first gear for people who don't check the forecast for permission. A focused catalog beats an endless one — every SKU earns its place.

Target customer

  • Weekend hikers & campers, 25–45, value-conscious but quality-driven
  • New-to-outdoors buyers who want one trusted shop, not 40 tabs
  • Gift buyers seeking sub-$100 functional gifts

Differentiators

Weather-first curation Sub-$100 entry prices Free shipping over $75 2-year warranty story Fast, lean operations

03 — Business Model

Lean, dropship-first

Phase 1 — Validate (dropship)

List curated products sourced from no-MOQ suppliers (AliExpress / CJdropshipping). Orders are fulfilled per-sale, so there's no upfront inventory cost. Goal: find the 2–3 winning SKUs.

Phase 2 — Scale (hybrid)

Bulk-buy proven winners for faster shipping and better margins; use a fulfillment/3PL or US-warehoused supplier. Add private-label packaging to build the Rainhawk brand.

Revenue = retail price − product cost − payment fees (~3%) − shipping subsidy. The catalog below is priced for a blended gross margin in the ~55–65% range before ad spend.

07 — Roadmap

From here to first revenue

Done Week 0
  • ✓ Brand, positioning & storefront built and deployed live
  • ✓ Catalog curated and sourced to real suppliers with links
  • ✓ Custom domain acquired and pointed at the site
Phase 1 Weeks 1–3
  • Connect a payment processor (Stripe / PayPal) for real checkout
  • Wire supplier fulfillment (AliExpress/CJ) + order-routing workflow
  • Shoot/collect real product imagery; finalize copy & policies (shipping, returns)
  • Set up analytics (GA4 / Plausible) + a Klaviyo email capture
Phase 2 Weeks 4–8
  • Soft launch to friends/family + a small paid test ($300–$500) on Meta/TikTok
  • Identify 2–3 winning SKUs by conversion & margin
  • Iterate product pages, add reviews/social proof, A/B test hero offers
Phase 3 Months 3–6
  • Bulk-buy winners; move to faster US-warehouse fulfillment
  • Introduce Rainhawk-branded packaging & first private-label SKU
  • Scale profitable ad channels; launch retention email/SMS flows
